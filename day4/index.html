Who does that?

Sloths are mammals noted for slowness of movement and for spending most of their lives hanging upside down in the trees of the tropical rain forests. It is their anatomy - that allows them to chill like this! In the schema on the right you can see which body parts allows sloths to be who they are.

Some sloth families, like Three-toed sloths family Bradypodidae, move in the same way but often sit in the forks of trees rather than hanging from branches. If you look along forest canopy, apart from sitting or hanging, you will see sloths also sunning, resting, or feeding on leaves.

If you go to the forest and do not see them - maybe they still actually are there. It can be that your eyes are defeating you! Sloths sleep either perched in the fork of a tree or hanging from a branch, with all four feet bunched together and the head tucked in on the chest. In this posture the sloth resembles a clump of dead leaves, so inconspicuous that it was once thought these animals ate only the leaves of cecropia trees because in other trees they went undetected.

Special Environment

The sloth family is big. There are many of them. Each one more cute than the other. Sloths like nice places. They live in nature. Actually, they spend almost all of their lives in trees. Here are some places where they have been seen:

  • Honduras
  • northern South America
  • northern Argentina
  • small Atlantic forest of southeastern Brazil
  • a small Caribbean island off the northwestern coast of Panama

    • Sloths like places in the tropical regions of the Earth, where they get plenty of sunlight year-round. It also rains there. Sometimes there is high and continuous rainfall, with annual rainfall in some places between 2.5 and 4.5 metres. Perhaps that is why slots chill in the trees. Just in case that rain is about to start and they can't make it on time to be high enough.

    How to climb trees

    To better understand how sloths climb the trees and hang in the trees, we need to understand the trees. A tree is a perennial plant with an elongated stem, supporting branches and leaves in most species. Again, in the schema on the right you can see the anatomy of the tree.

    Trees include a variety of plant species that have independently evolved a trunk and branches as a way to tower above other plants to compete for sunlight. The trunk typically contains woody tissue for strength, and vascular tissue to carry materials from one part of the tree to another. For most trees it is surrounded by a layer of bark which serves as a protective barrier.

    Below the ground, the roots branch and spread out widely; they serve to anchor the tree and extract moisture and nutrients from the soil.

    Sloths also code

    You wouldn't expect it, but think of their claws! Like in life, they do take their time in coding. Turns out, the three-toed sloths code a bit faster than two-toed sloths. Researchers have found weird symbols on the tree branches, and it wasn't until last year, that by coincidence, these symbols were decoded by developers! Below are the examples of code that was found on the trees.

    #!/bin/bash STR="Hello Sloth!" echo $STR
    int main() { std::cout << "Hello, sloth!\n"; return 0; }

    This is crazy, right? But it is real. Perhaps we underestimate these creatures just because they are so slow. We do have much more to learn about them!
